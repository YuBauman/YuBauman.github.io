Overview

The purpose of the project: Create a valuable digital gifting experience for a user who receives intangible gift from loved ones on distance due to studies abroad.
My role: The project was realised in a design team of two people so I participated in all levels of projects and design thinking process.
Final product: Digital and physical prototypes thar represent valuable gifting experience for persona

Process

The project consisted of two major parts, where the first part consisted of emphathize and define modes, while the second part was ideate, prototype and test.

Emphathize

As a design team, first we established research methods and research questions that were suitable for our purpose.

Research methods:

  • Informal interview
  • Ethnographic interview
  • Observation
  • Exploration of artifacts
  • Desk research

Research questions:

  1. What helps the user to feel close with family and friends back home?
  2. What are experiences that the user had with intangible gifts
  3. What are the factors that make the gifting experience memorable for the user
  4. Which digital platforms/applications play an important role in their life
  5. What are core values for the user when preparing a gift for friends and family during holidays/celebration

As the set-up was ready, each member of the design team member gathered data on the assigned user according to the methods and research questions. First I had informal interview with my user to create relaxed atmosphere and get used to each other before we get down to business. For the second formal interview I prepared questions about user's personality, his surrounding, his values and his preferences and experiences with gifting. For observation I visited user's house and observed him during ineterviews. As a part of observation I also prepared a gift for a user for Dutch holiday Sinterklaas, so I could see the user in the gift receiving environment and how he behaves.

Define

After data gathering it was time to establish user insights and his needs. As a design team, we combined our findings and made persona and design vision based on it.

Persona

Design vision

Design statement gifting experience

The experience we want our user to have is to feel competent through overcoming a challenge, be engaged by the playfulness and humorous nature of the gift, and feel connected and supported by his friends.

UX factors

Activation and Immersion, since our user likes playing video games, he needs to be engaged through activation and elements of surprise, experience should be playful and fun. The user also likes to be challenged in order to get rewards. Our user likes to learn new skills and explore.
Relatedness, because our user is very close with his friends, and now, when he is studying in The Hague, far from his home, he misses having fun together with his friends. He needs to feel that they don't forget him and that they are as close as they have always been.
Competence, because our user works on his skills in order to feel better about himself. Achieving goals and learning new skills makes his self-confidence grow.

Ideate

For ideate part different creative techniques were used in order to come up with different untrivial ideas. First, I participated in brainstorm sessions with my partner. We used creative technique of forced connections to diverge ideas. For diverging part I came up with 30+ different ideas how to cover user's needs according to UX factors. Each idea was sketched. After diverging I switched to converging to choose the best ideas or creating new ones combining several sketches. To improve ideas we used storyboards and dramatic arc for our concepts.

At the end, my partner and I combined our design concepts and settled the final design team concept for prototyping.

Design team concept

Token

A mysterious book with the title “The ultimate technique of making tea” with a Royal ring and a letter. The Queen of England together with all the tea in England are kidnapped. The letter is from the evil teaglar who challenges you to come and catch him... if you can.

Digital unwrapping

  1. User's friends come to the Hague with private jet to take him to the mission for rescuing the Queen and tea. Each of them has specific power like +5 strength, +5 speed etc. As they arrive at the place where the Queen is held, they see the path of tea leaves.
  2. They meet the first person to fight who is a ninja, so he is very fast. The user fights with his fast friend. They beat him and gets a diamond. User's power - to steal powers/skills. For fighting a ninja, he gets a skill of being super-fast. With each challenge one friend stays behind and the user faces the villain alone. There is also character development, increase of level as the lowest to the highest after killing the enemy.
  3. They enter the enemy base. They find a locked up old man who identifies himself as the queen’s advisor. The old man says that they are keeping the sword Excalibur with him in the cell and that they need it to defeat the villain and safe the queen. They solve a puzzle and rescue the man.
  4. They enter the boss layer and face the maze separating them from the queen and villain. The queen shouts to follow the trail she secretly left, and the user has to reach the villain alone. The user defeats the villain and uses the sword to break out the queen. As a reward the Queen gives them a treasure box. When user opens it, there is his gift for his birthday.

Test

During the project I conducted user testing four times including tests of individual and design team protototypes. The testing was made both with gamers and non-gamers. Gamers were there to test immersion and activation, while non-gamers were needed to test ease-of-use. The idea behind it was that if even non-gamers will not have usability problems then gamers will understand the interface for sure.
After each user testing there was a circle of iterations according to feedback, both of the narrative or UI of the prototype. The users were included in testing since early stages of prototyping what allowed to identify issues from the beginning and react proactively. Since for the prototyping we had less than two weeks, it was important to test from the beginning not to waste time for something that would not work.
That's why I switched to hi-fi prototypes only after several circled or testing of lo-fi prototypes.

Conclusions

The project took two months and during this time I went through all the stages of design thinking process together with my partner. At the end we had engaging and very personalised gifting experience for our persona. The prototype includes user's friends and they go on adventure together that covers UX factors immersion and relatedness. At the same time, a user was activated through the rewarding system included in a game and felt competent because of overcoming challenges. The project introduced to me the whole circle of design process and allowed me to experience work in a design team.
