Overview

The purpose of the project: Examine Rijksstudio website usability, reveal usability issues, provide recommendations for user experience improvement.
My role: Active participation in all levels of group work (set-up, data analysis, conclusions), individual testing and interviewing.
Final product: Research journal, recommendations for website usability improvement.

Process

For this project we worked in a group of 5 people. Since we all had to conduct website testing and interviews with the participants, the first step was to create the set-up and the protocol. First, we examined the website ourselves and shared our opinion, which usability issues we noticed. We came to the conclusion that Rijksstudio is supposed to work as art community and social platform, so we wanted to test how well it works. We also wanted to test how users experience the flow of the website anf its UI elements. Based on this we established research questions that covered the usability aspects we were interested in.
We established the tasks the participant have to execute during testing, the set-up and tools. It was very important that we all follow the protocol so the results wouldn’t depend on our discrepancies. We also had the list of questions for the interview that we all had to ask our participants after the testing.

Testing

The testing with participants was conducted individually by all the project group members. It was our responsibility to find testers and conduct professional user testing. During the testing participants had to complete six life-like tasks like create the account or find a specific piece of art to add it to their collection.
I had the testing with an art-lover who enjoys visiting museums in different countries. The testing took place in Teams and was recorded. During the testing I observed user’s actions, emotions and comments and added the notes in the protocol. After the testing I conducted an interview with the participant where I asked him a list of questions prepared in advance, but also follow-up questions in order to understand the reasoning and his impression better.

Research

After all of us conducted the testing it was the time to process gathered data in order to come up with the conclusions. For this we used different techniques of data visualisation that were used per task and research question. We used data cards and clusters, colour codes for efficient/inefficient actions of users, the chain of participants actions to examine the flow.

Data visualisation allowed us to go through all the results we had from several tests and reveal the patterns, similarities and come to conclusions based on it.
As a result, we had the table of usability problems, visualisation of smoothness and interruptions of the flow, the list of UI elements that confuse users and finally the general opinion and impressions of the website’s purpose and functions.

Conclusions and Recommendations

At the end of the project we worked on the recommendations based on our conclusions. We pointed out which UI elements are confusing for users and has to be changed since it interrupts the flow. We also mentioned the functions users might miss at the website as clickable logo to navigate to the homepage. It was also recommended to follow users’ mental models and make use of them in order to avoid confusion.
We came up with the recommendation to make the account creating process more interesting since users found it boring and concluded that the website about the art could make it more creative.
After all, since we tested how well the website fits the role of social platform, we came up with several recommendations how to make it work as social platform and create an art community, since at the moment the website didn’t quite fit this role.

My role

During the project I actively participated in all the activities and discussions. I helped to come up with ideas for research questions and testing tasks. I was responsible for making the testing protocol from scratch, that included the rules of testing, introduction, the tables with tasks and notes, interview set-up.
I individually conducted the testing and the interview with my participant and processed all the gathered data. After testing I actively participated in review of the results and discussions about the conclusions and recommendation, after what I was responsible for writing the recommendation part of our research journal.
The grading of the project was divided into group and individual effort. For individual part I received the highest possible grade and particular complements about my interviewing skills. I found this project very interesting and productive to work on essential skills of the UX designer as research skills, interviewing skills and ability to process gathered data and come up with valuable recommendations and solutions.
